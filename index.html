Chika Maduabuchi

I am a graduate Machine Learning researcher at the MIT Red Lab, working under the supervision of Professors Matteo Bucci and Ericmoore Jossou. My research interests revolve around predictive machine learning and large vision-language models towards developing generalizable multimodal AI for social good.

Before joining MIT, I received my Bachelor of Engineering degree at the University of Nigeria Nsukka fortunate to be supervised by Professors Chigbo Mgbemene, Howard Njoku, and Chigbogu Ozoegwu. After that, I founded the Artificial Intelligence Laboratory where I lead research on developing AI models for social good.

Featured Projects

BoilSAM

Inspired by the Segment Anything Model developed by Meta AI, I am currently developing foundation models for zero-shot generalization on large scale boiling datasets. This will enable accurate real-time segmentation to facilitate autonomous operation in various industries.

SGPT2

I have developed Small GPT2 (SGPT2), a model with 0.2 M parameters fine-tuned on over 40 K lines of Shakespearean text. After proper fine-tuning, the model was able to generate novel text indistinguishable from Shakespearean text. The code and dataset used can be found on GitHub.

DDPM

Similarly, I have worked on developing Denoising Diffusion Probabilistic Models (DDPM) for generating high-fidelity images. This project explores the potentials of diffusion models in creating realistic images and their applications in various domains. More details can be found on GitHub.

RLPG

Exploring reinforcement learning, I developed models that utilize policy gradients to solve complex decision-making tasks. This project highlights the use of reinforcement learning in navigating environments and optimizing strategies for goal achievement. See the project on GitHub.

ML4Solar

My work on ML4Solar involves using machine learning algorithms to optimize solar energy collection and usage. This project aims at improving the efficiency and reliability of solar power systems through intelligent data analysis and predictive modeling. Project details are available on GitHub.
